Visual Hierarchy

Apple

apple.com

Visual Hierarchy is one of the most important principles behind good web design. It’s the order in which the human eye perceives what it sees. In apple.com the eye-catcher is the image of the iPhone 13 pro.
The visual hierarchy, in this case, is very simple to define and other elements like the links Learn more and Buy are very well positioned on the webpage, invite the possible customers to know more about the product or just go ahead and buy it.
They get more attention them other parts of the webpage. The business object is clear "Sell iPhone 13 Pro", a lanching product.

Law of Simetry

Nuances Requinte

nuancesrequinte.com

Although alignment is typically overlooked, without this tool a design would lack a sense of direction, unity, or organization. Alignment simply refers to how elements are placed on a page. Its purpose goes beyond the organization, it is vital for enhancing the overall visual appeal of a design or page layout.
The alignment of the objects in the page nuancesrequinte.com refers mainly to the buttons ad the call. The page shows a balance among the elements directing possible clients to be distributors or salon professionals.

White Space Clean Design

Google

google.com

White space (also called “negative space”) is the portion of a web page that remains “empty.” It’s the space between graphics, margins, gutters, space between columns, space between lines of type, or visuals.
It is an important element of webdesign. The google page it is a excelent example of how white space can be used. There is no distrantion and the objective is clear and direct, easy way to search things on the web. The right amount of white space turn the google website clean design and interects with user in the easiest way.
